The Met. Office - Aims and Objectives

The Met. Office provides weather and climate-related services to a wide range of customers. We are well known to the public through weather forecasts broadcast on television and radio but also provide specialised services, such as those in support of the Armed Forces and civil aviation, warnings of severe weather events and advice to the Government on climate change. Weather intelligence can also be used to advantage in business planning and operations.

The formal aim and objectives shown below are taken from the Framework Document (1990) of The Met. Office Executive Agency. The document has been revised and is awaiting approval; only minor changes have been proposed.

Aim

The aim of The Met. Office Executive Agency is to provide for United Kingdom military and civil users an effective, modern and efficient National Meteorological Service.

Objectives

In fulfilling its aim, the principal objectives of The Met. Office shall be to:

  • provide a range of meteorological services to meet the requirements specified by the British Armed Forces and the Ministry of Defence;
  • provide under contract meteorological services to the Civil Aviation Authority;
  • make available warnings, forecasts and other meteorological services to shipping, the general public and others as may be required by the Secretary of State for Defence;
  • offer, and provide on payment, meteorological services to other Government Departments, commerce and industry;
  • provide information and advice to Ministers as required by them on matters related to meteorology;
  • represent and maintain British interests within the World Meteorological Organization and other relevant international bodies;
    and
  • maintain an up-to-date National Meteorological Library and Archive.

In meeting these objectives, The Met. Office will:

  • aim to achieve progressively more-demanding quality of service and efficiency targets;
  • pursue research to attain those levels of capability and expertise necessary to meet its objectives economically and on repayment to meet customer requirements;
  • develop and pursue profitable commercial outlets for its services within Departmental guidelines;
  • maintain and renew the buildings and equipment needed for its operation in the light of forward plans agreed with the Ministry of Defence;
    and
  • ensure that it recruits, trains and retains the right level and mix of personnel to meet its objectives in accordance with good employer practices.
