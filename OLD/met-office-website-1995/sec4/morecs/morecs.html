Met. Office Rainfall and Evaporation Calculation System - MORECS

The only operational nationwide service giving assessments of rainfall, evaporation and soil moisture in real time.

At the heart of the MORECS system is a complicated equation which calculates the soil moisture and evaporation values from measurements of temperature, sunshine, wind and humidity. The equation also allows for local variations in soil type and the crops being grown on the land.

MORECS calculates the soil state and evaporation for both a 40 km by 40 km grid nation-wide on a weekly operational basis and at individual weather recording sites for hindsight studies. The operational MORECS results can be automatically faxed to customers or sent by post. Some examples of the uses of MORECS include:

Water Authorities

  • Helping to predict river flow and input to river flow models.
  • Predicting evaporation from open reservoirs

Environment

  • Estimating leachate from landfill sites.

Insurance

  • Helping in the servicing of subsidence claims

Agriculture

  • Assessing drought stress in crops.
  • Ensuring chemicals are applied when the soil moisture is most suitable.
  • Balance sheet irrigation scheduling by combining evaporation figures with rainfall measurements.
  • Trafficability: single station MORECS can help assess the value of low ground-pressure tyres.
