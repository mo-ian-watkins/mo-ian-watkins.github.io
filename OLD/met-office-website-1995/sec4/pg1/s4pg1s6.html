Defence

The Defence Services Branch of The Met.Office is one the Office's largest branches employing over 500 people: 19 in the `policy' unit in Bracknell and the remainder at more than 50 operational RAF, Army and PE Range stations both in the UK and abroad.

As well as providing on site forecasting services the Defence branch also offers consultancy and research services to MOD and Defence Agency staff such as:

    Tactical Decision Aids - used to predict the performance of weapon systems and sensors because, for example, laser guided bombs, night vision goggles and infrared sensors are all affected by the weather.

    Radio and Microwave Propagation - The Met.Office has access to state-of-the-art models to assist in tactical decisions in the presence of `radar ducts' which affect radar performance.

    Electro-optics - Further models are used to forecast night illumination levels to support the use of night vision goggles and forward looking infrared.

    Acoustic Propagation - Operational models have been developed to help ranges predict the transmission of noise to local populations.

    Field Support - Operational services are supplied to Armed Forces in the field, in places such as Bosnia, via Mobile Meteorological Units which can travel at very short notice to scenes of conflict complete with deployable satellite reception systems and an Outstation Display System for presenting all the relevant meteorological information.
