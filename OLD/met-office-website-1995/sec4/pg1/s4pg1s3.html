Aviation

The Met.Office provides services to the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA), which is the UK Met. Authority for Aviation, and provides tailored services to commercial organisations world-wide. The Met.Office is one of only two World Area Forecast Centres appointed by the International Civil Aviation Organisation (ICAO) through the British government and the CAA, providing global forecasts of upper winds and temperatures for all flights throughout the world.

Under the direction of the CAA The Met.Office provides low level forecasts for aviation over the UK and offshore waters. A basic service agreed with the CAA provides information to enable all pilots, whether flying for pleasure or for commercial benefits to self brief before take off.

To enable private pilots, smaller aerodromes and flying clubs to fly safely, with confidence and to plan more economically The Met.Office provides tailored weather information by phone, fax and PC.

    MIST The one-stop source for all aviation weather, MIST provides accurate, up-to-date weather on your PC in an interactive graphical format with coloured display. Designed especially for flying organisations and airfields.

    Mini-MIST Easy to access, accurate up-to-date weather information is provided in text format direct from The Met.Office on your own PC. Tailored especially for the private pilot.

    MetFAX Aviation A premium rate fax service providing concise accurate weather information in a cost effective graphic and text format. Suitable for all flyers. There is a subscription MetFAX for overseas customers.

    Broadcast Fax Ideal for aviation organisations with regular flights, Broadcast Fax is a faxed weather service sent routinely from The Met.Office in an accurate and up-to-date format.

    Direct Access Forecast Services Designed for pilots who need specialist briefings before making critical planning decisions, the Direct access Forecast Service offers personal contact with a Met.Office forecaster for all your route forecasts and planning needs.

    Aviation Met. Training Courses providing a thorough grounding in aviation meteorology enabling pilots to understand and interpret the forecasts and implications are conducted at The Met.Office College in Berkshire.
