Web Site Information

This site runs from a Hewlett Packard 9000/715 workstation using NCSA HTTPd. Most of the pages were written within a four-week period. The site is best viewed using a graphical browser running at a screen resolution of 640x480 and 256 colours or better. If graphics are not required you can also use Lynx (or similar) as ALT tags have been used where required.

Creating this site has directly and indirectly involved many in The Met.Office. Thanks to all the people whose words we stole, whose time we hijacked and in whose footsteps we trod.

Web site team

Felicity Bellini - Graphics (doodled in her paint package)
"There are many restrictions when designing pages for the Internet; image size and resolution, number of graphics, colour and type... These pages are a compromise between the original designs and what could actually be written. Our site not only looks good, but is hopefully easy to use; the Home page should help you locate any information you require. The buttons and graphics were initially created in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop on the Apple Macintosh. Having saved them as BMP files (68 dpi, 8-bit, indexed colour), they were then converted to GIFs on the PC using CorelDraw. The programmer then strutted his stuff with HTML to produce the finished article you see here."

James Barrott - Project Manager (hassled people for site content)
"To create this site I had to use guile, flattery and persuasion to assemble a big heap of information about meteorology and The Met. Office. This was heaved over to Felicity and Ian who knitted it into the tasteful construction in which you stand."

Ian Watkins - Technical Co-ordinator (kludged together the HTML)
"The site was built with a tree structure in mind as this allowed the use of a toolbar at the bottom of each page. However, because this is hypertext, some pages are not on the tree, therefore, on these pages a full toolbar isn't available. I used a PC to build this site and my toolkit includes PFE (Programmers File Editor) and PaintShop Pro v3.11. A workstation was only used to test the forms and cgi-bin scripts."
